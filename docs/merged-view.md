# Merged view pivot

The account > addressbook > contacts hierarchy is too rigid: seeing another addressbook means navigating out and back in, moving a card between accounts has no home, and the app opens on a list of containers instead of the contacts themselves. The pivot turns the app contact-first: one merged list across every subscribed account and addressbook, the containers demoted to a filter and a management screen. The Android Contacts app is the precedent, with the one design decision that matters borrowed from it: **merged is a view, not a storage model**. The native app never merges storage; every raw contact belongs to exactly one account and the merged contact is an aggregation layer on top. Cardamum does the same.

## Model

Three concepts, replacing "a card belongs to multiple addressbooks and accounts":

- **Replica**: one server resource, the unit of storage, staging and sync: (account, card id, vCard, etag, staged flags). Every card row is bound to exactly one remote; even an offline-created card is born assigned to its target account and book, a replica whose server resource does not exist yet.
- **Membership**: the addressbooks a replica belongs to *within its account*. JMAP (`addressBookIds`) and Google (group labels) are natively m:n; CardDAV and Graph constrain the set to one collection. Membership is a field on the replica, never a duplication of it.
- **Link**: the cross-account identity tying replicas into one logical contact. Keyed by the vCard UID (copying a card to another account preserves it), extended by manual link/unlink later. Links exist only in the view layer: nothing on any server and nothing in sync depends on them.

The invariant: **storage and sync speak only in replicas (one card row, one remote resource); the logical multi-account contact exists only as a link between replicas at display time.** Sync stays the per-account, per-book loop it is today, each replica reconciling under its own etag/guard; there is no multi-master anything. A push conflict (the remote changed under a staged local change) resolves per replica with the vcard-rs three-way merge against the staged base: the local side wins same-field collisions, every other remote change flows in, an update beats a removal, and the merged card pushes against the fresh server state. The UX on top is merged all the way: one row, one edit form, and saving fans the form model out as ordinary staged edits, one per replica, each applied to that replica's own document (so its UID and unmanaged properties stay its own). Divergence between linked replicas is a display state, not a sync conflict: it resolves through the same form, prefilled with the union of the documents (the vcard-rs three-way merge against an empty base) and per-field pickers on the values that disagree.

## Stages

### Stage 1: contact-first home (implemented)

The app opens on the merged contacts list across every subscribed addressbook of every account. UID-equal cards collapse into one row (flagged with the account count). The old home becomes the Addressbooks management screen behind an app-bar icon: same account groups, account add/delete, and one subscription checkbox per book, the whole row its tap target, toggling instantly and without confirmation (the store and the phone projection catch up on the next sync passes). Adding a contact always asks for the target addressbook when more than one is subscribed: an explicit choice beats a default landing cards where the user does not expect them. Multi-select delete removes every replica of the selected rows.

### Stage 2: replica storage (implemented)

CardStore (schema v6) keys cards by account plus a backend-unique key, with the addressbooks a replica belongs to in a separate membership table: a JMAP card in two books or a Google card under several labels is one row instead of per-book duplicates. On the account-level backends the key is the bare server id; on CardDAV and Graph resource ids are only unique per collection, so the collection URL is part of the key (and the membership is always exactly one). Sync now loops accounts: JMAP and Google fetch every card once (JMAP `ContactCard/query` unfiltered with `addressBookIds`, Google `connections.list` with group memberships), map the memberships onto the subscribed addressbooks and skip cards with none; CardDAV and Graph keep their per-collection cycle. Google contact groups are surfaced as addressbooks (myContacts as Contacts, then the user groups), replacing the single synthetic Contacts book, and X-GOOGLE-MEMBERSHIP is no longer minted (memberships are structural now; legacy lines are still consumed). Memberships are read-only in this stage: fetches refresh them as server truth, pushes never touch them, and a Google create lands in myContacts regardless of the chosen book (group assignment needs the stage 3 membership operations).

### Stage 3: transparent linking (implemented, apart from match suggestions)

Linking is not a user concept: cards sharing a vCard UID are one contact, always, with no link, unlink, copy or move verbs to learn. A contact backed by several physical cards just shows a small link glyph and the card count next to its name. The link and detached tables stay in the store as internal mechanics (the future match suggestions will use them), but nothing in the UI manages them.

Placement is one gesture, the editor's addressbooks dialog: checked means the contact exists there, through any of its cards. Checking a book adds the contact there: a staged membership when the contact already has a card on that account-level backend (pushed as one JMAP `addressBookIds` patch, or Google `contactGroups.members.modify` calls per group; membership changes stage in the store and push after the cards, so a created card's staged memberships ride its server-assigned id), a copied card sharing the vCard UID anywhere else, merging into the same contact transparently. Unchecking removes it: a staged membership removal when the card keeps other books, a staged delete of the card otherwise; the last remaining book is locked (a contact nowhere is a delete, not a move). Moving is checking one book and unchecking another.

One bottom-up combine gesture remains, **Merge** in the list's selection mode, because two cards that are the same person but were born independently carry different UIDs and would never group on their own: the user picks the addressbook whose copy survives (skipped when every card shares one book), the merge form opens on the union of the documents (its list entries deduplicated by their identifying fields: merging reformats, unlike faithful single-card editing), and saving stages the merged content onto the survivor and a delete for every other card. Cards already carrying the same content absorb directly, form-free. The accepted trade-off of having no unlink: a pathological UID collision (two genuinely different contacts sharing a UID, from a buggy client or import) fuses permanently; the "Diverged" flag and the merge form's chips at least make it visible, and a detach gesture can quietly return if it ever bites. The conservative, always-confirmed match suggestions landed as the semi-automatic duplicate remover (its own app-bar icon, next to the overflow menu): the bridge groups cards on exact normalized email, phone (trailing digits, so a country code does not split a match) or full-name equality, never grouping cards that already share a UID, and each group is reviewed one dialog at a time with three verbs: Ignore (the dismissal persists in the store, the group is never proposed again), Merge (the normal merge flow above), and Link (only when every card lives in its own addressbook: the cards become one contact by staging a shared UID, servers enforcing UID uniqueness per collection).

### Stage 4: merged editing and divergence (implemented, superseding pick-a-source)

Tapping a merged row opens one edit form for the whole contact, chooser-free. When the replicas agree, the form is a plain edit of their shared content; when they diverge, the bridge merges the distinct documents through the vcard-rs three-way merge against an empty base (identical properties land once, multi-valued properties union, a divergent single-valued property keeps the first document's value) and the form shows one tappable chip per alternative value under each conflicted field (present-vs-absent counts too: the empty state rides along as a pickable alternative, so a field one card carries and another lacks never yields a blank conflict view). Saving fans out: the form model applies to every replica's own document through the ordinary vCard patch, so each replica keeps its UID and unmanaged properties (minted X-* props, provider stashes, PHOTO) while the managed content converges; replicas whose content already matches are skipped, and every staged edit pushes through its own account's normal path (etag guards and 412 handling included). No merged document is ever stored or synced.

Divergence follows the same rule: the bridge indexes every card at write time (display fields plus a normalized content hash, FNV-1a over the canonical field model with sorted arrays and the per-replica identity and unmanaged data excluded), the store persists the index as columns, and a merged row whose replicas' hashes differ carries a danger icon in its trailing slot (where the selection checkbox otherwise rides). Resolution is just opening and saving the contact: the editor opens in conflict mode, showing only the disagreeing fields (the chipped singles plus the list sections the bridge reports as changed), the same flag next to its title, and its save button reads "Resolve conflict"; the hidden fields keep their merged values. Since every in-app save fans out, divergence only ever comes from outside: another client editing one copy, or a backend reshaping managed fields on refetch. A remote edit reaching one copy of a multi-account contact therefore always shows as a conflict to resolve; propagating the unambiguous case automatically (one side changed, every other copy still matches the old content) is a possible future refinement, pimdir territory. Still to come, nothing: fan-out on save made the per-link "keep in sync" toggle pointless (it is the default), and unlink is the escape hatch for deliberately divergent copies.

A contact backed by several physical cards shows a link glyph and the card count next to its name. (Colour badges per addressbook were tried and removed; per-book colours and display names may come back later as a settings page.)

### Stage 5: the local book and the two-axis addressbook model (implemented)

A built-in, undeletable local account "This app" with one addressbook "Local contacts" makes the app usable with no account at all: onboarding is never forced, the merged list opens on the (empty) local book, and its cards live only in the app's store. It is synthesized in memory (never in the credential store) and seeded into the addressbook table on launch, so a fresh install or a schema rebuild always finds it present and subscribed. Its `local://` base URL keeps it out of every backend transport (Native.accountInfo reports a transport-less `local` backend, non-account-level), so the remote sync skips it.

Every addressbook now carries two independent switches, replacing the single subscription that used to also drive the phone projection:

- **Subscribe**: its contacts are visible in the merged list and take part in sync. The local book is always subscribed (locked on).
- **Sync to phone**: it is projected into the phone's Contacts app as its own account (the existing per-book Android-account spoke). Phone sync requires the subscription, so a book reaches the Contacts app only when both are on. Off by default, including for the local book: turn it on to push those cards to the phone under their own account name (the local book projects as "Local contacts").

This decouples "synced with the server" from "on the phone" and matches how the OS Contacts app organises contacts by account, so a Gmail book shows as Gmail on the phone and a Posteo book as Posteo, instead of a single flattened pile. The addressbooks live in a real navigation drawer (androidx.drawerlayout, the one Jetpack concession, opened by the contacts burger): accounts are bold non-foldable section headers over their books, each book row opens its settings dialog (the two switches; the book name is the dialog title), a phone glyph marks a mirrored book and a dimmed row an unsubscribed one, and the drawer header carries the add-account plus. There is one Sync button (no remote/local dropdown): it runs the remote exchange for every subscribed book, then the phone projection for every mirrored book, and asks the contacts permission only when something actually projects.

The local book is a normal, listed entry in the editor's addressbooks dialog and the guaranteed home of the placement model: unchecking every book force-checks "Local contacts" and locks it, so a contact always lives somewhere without a nagging message (Delete is the explicit removal). Checking another book re-enables it and clears the forced check. Saving applies the desired state through the same staged-membership / copy / removal machinery as any book. The create-target chooser lists "Local contacts" alongside the rest, so a new contact can be born on the device directly.

## Non-goals

- **Multi-master sync**: a logical contact never pushes anywhere; only replicas do.
- **A merged document**: the merged contact is a projection; saving applies the form model per replica, and nothing merged is ever stored or synced.
- **Automatic aggregation**: no name-based heuristics; UID equality and explicit user actions only (Android's auto-aggregation false positives are the cautionary tale).
